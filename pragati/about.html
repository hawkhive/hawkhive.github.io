Our Story

How It All Began

A story of passion, perseverance, and purpose

The Early Days – 1998

It was 1998 when F S Bhatt started his sub‑broking office in Idar and later moved to Himatnagar in 2002 and registered with the Securities and Exchange Board of India(SEBI). Computers were rare, and trading happened on noisy terminals. The office was a small room filled with two partners, one employee, and a crowd of over 25 clients shouting buy/sell orders from behind. Four people constantly banging keyboards, phones ringing non‑stop, and over 300 active clients. That chaotic energy built the foundation of Pragati Financial.

"Those days, we didn't have fancy charts – just raw instinct, hard work, and relationships."

The Inspiration That Changed Everything

When Sahil Bhatt was 8 years old, he read a small newspaper column about Warren Buffett – how he started investing young, bought his first stock at 11, and built wealth through patience. That story sparked a fire. Sahil started asking his father endless questions about stocks. With his pocket money, he'd ask F S Bhatt to buy shares on his behalf. His first profit? He bought a bicycle with it.

That bicycle wasn't just a ride – it was proof that small investments could lead to real rewards.

Meet the Duo Behind Pragati

30+ years of combined wisdom – traditional roots, modern vision

F S Bhatt – Founder & Stock Sub‑Broker

28+ years of experience. He's seen it all – the 2008 crash, the 2020 pandemic, several bull runs. Started when there were hardly computers, working with two partners and one employee in a room full of traders. Phones rang all day, over 300 clients, and the energy was electric. That resilience and market wisdom is at the core of every recommendation today.

Specializes in Equity, F&O, and risk-first trading.

Sahil Bhatt – Mutual Fund Distributor & Educator

Learned stock markets from his father since age 8. But he wanted to explore the real world first – so he studied Marine Engineering and worked on ships. After sailing the oceans, he returned home to join his father, bringing fresh energy. He revived the business, added Mutual Funds, Insurance, and Financial Literacy programs.

His philosophy: "Investing in your youth isn't optional – it's a must. I don't care about commissions; I care about how much wealth I can help you build."

Friends joke, "Muje bakra mil gaya halal karne ke liye" – but I'm dead serious about their financial future.
