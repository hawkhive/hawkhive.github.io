Types of Mutual Funds

Choose based on your risk appetite and investment horizon

Equity Funds

Invest primarily in stocks. High return potential with higher risk. Ideal for long-term wealth creation (5+ years).

Debt Funds

Invest in bonds, treasury bills. Lower risk, stable returns. Suitable for short to medium term (1-3 years).

Hybrid Funds

Mix of equity and debt. Balanced risk-return profile. Great for moderate risk takers.

ELSS (Tax Saving)

Section 80C deduction up to ₹1.5 lakh. Lock-in period of 3 years. Equity-oriented.

Sectoral/Thematic

Focus on specific sectors like IT, Pharma, Banking. High risk, high reward potential.

Index Funds

Passive funds tracking Nifty 50, Sensex. Low expense ratio, good for long term.

SIP vs Lump Sum

Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)

Invest fixed amount monthly. Rupee cost averaging, disciplined approach. Ideal for salaried individuals. Start from ₹500.

Lump Sum

One-time investment of larger amount. Suitable when you have surplus cash. Market timing matters more.

Key Benefits

  • Professional Management – Expert fund managers handle your money.
  • Diversification – Spread across sectors & companies.
  • Liquidity – Easy redemption (except ELSS lock-in).
  • Tax Efficiency – ELSS saves tax, LTCG taxed at 12.5% above ₹1.25L.
  • Affordability – Start with as low as ₹500 via SIP.

Taxation on Mutual Funds

  • ✔ Equity Funds: LTCG (>1 year) 12.5% above ₹1.25L; STCG 20%.
  • ✔ Debt Funds: LTCG (>3 years) 20% with indexation; STCG as per income slab.
  • ✔ ELSS: Tax deduction u/s 80C up to ₹1.5L (3 years lock-in).
  • ✔ Dividends are added to income and taxed as per slab.
